The problem

Every day, thousands of naira change hands over DMs — with no protection on either side.

Buyers take blind risks

Sending money to a vendor you found on Instagram — with no receipt, no recourse, and no way back if things go wrong — is still the default for most online shopping in Nigeria.

Honest sellers lose sales

Trustworthy vendors lose first-time buyers every day because there is no way to tell them apart from bad actors. Without a trust layer, hesitation wins and the sale is lost.

Trust is broken by default

The DM-to-transfer pipeline is fast but fragile. One bad experience spreads widely, and everyone pays the price — including the vendors who always deliver.

How it works

Four steps. Full protection.

OKO-TO sits between the DM and the transfer — keeping both the buyer and the seller safe throughout.

01

Seller creates a protected link

The seller sets the item, price, and delivery terms, then generates a unique OKO-TO payment link in seconds. No app download needed for either party.

02

Buyer pays through the link

The buyer clicks the link, reviews the agreed terms, and completes payment. The payment is protected until the buyer confirms delivery or the agreed release condition is met.

03

Seller delivers the item

The seller ships or delivers, knowing the payment is secured. The buyer knows exactly what was agreed before anything moves.

04

Buyer confirms. Funds are released.

Once the buyer confirms delivery, the funds are released to the seller. If either side has a concern, a dispute can be raised and reviewed fairly.
